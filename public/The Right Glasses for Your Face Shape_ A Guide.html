The Right Glasses for Your Face Shape: A Guide

Choosing the right glasses is not just a matter of style, but also of face shape. The perfect glasses can highlight your best features and give your face a harmonious appearance. In this blog post, we'll show you which glasses shapes best suit the six most common face shapes: oblong, oval, square, round, diamond and heart-shaped.

Oblong Face Shape

If you have an oblong face, it's best to choose glasses that make your face appear wider. Wide glasses with square or round lenses can help to visually shorten the face and create balance. Avoid narrow or rectangular glasses, as they can make your face appear longer.

Oval Face Shape

Oval faces are considered the "ideal" face shape as they are well-proportioned. If you have an oval face, you're lucky because almost any glasses shape will suit you. You can experiment with different styles and shapes, but make sure the glasses are not wider than the widest part of your face.

Round Face Shape

Round faces are often as wide as they are long, with full cheeks and a round chin line. To make your face appear longer and slimmer, rectangular or square glasses are ideal. They help to soften the round features and visually elongate the face.

Square Face Shape

Square faces often have prominent features such as a strong jaw and a wide forehead. To soften these features and make the face appear softer, round or oval glasses are a good choice. Avoid angular glasses as they can emphasize the hard lines of your face.

Heart-shaped Face Shape

Heart-shaped faces are wide at the forehead and taper to a pointed chin. To maintain balance and make the forehead appear less dominant, glasses with round or oval lenses and a thin frame are a good choice. Avoid glasses that are wide at the top, as they can emphasize the forehead and make the face appear unbalanced.

Diamond Face Shape

Diamond-shaped faces have a narrow forehead and jawline with the cheekbones being the widest part of the face, and a pointed chin. To balance the face, choose glasses with round or oval lenses and a thin frame. Avoid glasses that are too wide at the top, as they can make the face appear unbalanced.

Choosing the right glasses can make a big difference in your appearance. Remember, it's not just about style, but also about what suits your face shape. With this guide, you can find the perfect glasses for your face shape and take your look to the next level.
